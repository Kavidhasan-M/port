Duration

5 weeks

A team building platform for students, where the students can find people who interested to work with them on an idea or a project.

Problem

Students have trouble finding people who are interested to work as team with them on their idea.

When students want to work on any projects with other students, there is no way to find people to work with, or available projects to join when they don't have an idea themselves.

My contribution

  • Conducted user interviews
  • Made sketches and build prototypes

Initial stage

One day, I faced a problem to find a backend developer for my project and I didn't expect him/her to be an expert. Later, I found a guy who can do backend from my department. I spoke to him about the problem that I faced before finding him. We teamed up and started reaching out students in who face the same problem. I prepared a questionnaire for the students who had gone through the same trouble and conducted interview with them.

Insights

  • There is no direct way to find teammates to work on projects.
  • Not all students want their teammates to be an expert at the expected role.
  • Students have some basic skills on something which cannot be considered as good at that.
  • Those skills are not tried out until the moment comes.

Userflow

Features

Depending on the tasks that users will carry out on this platform, the following tasks are implemented as features,

  • Explore the projects available to join in the team.
  • Search people based on skills.
  • Post about the project they are working on or about to work with roles available to join the team.
  • Status about their request to the team builder.

Iterations

Iteration 1

Before we had CTA buttons for signup and login on the landing page. After finding that the users hesitates to create an account, we allowed users to explore the projects available in the platform. The users need to signup if they are interested to join in a team.

Iteration 2

Finding first set of users

I started reaching out to students who want to build a team for the project they are working on or about to work on and spoke to them about this platform. I asked them to create a post for their project then we found the user traction.
